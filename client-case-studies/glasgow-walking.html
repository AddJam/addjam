Future City Glasgow: Glasgow Walking

A product for Android, iOS and Web

Making Glasgow a more active city

Add Jam delivered mobile applications for the Active Travel stream of the £24 million Innovate UK funded Future City project in Glasgow. We developed Glasgow Walking for iOS and Android aimed at making information on city attractions accessible which in turn would encourage visitors and citizens to get up and explore our beautiful home city.

As little as 5% increase in activity from each citizen is estimated to save the NHS £60 million by reducing the risk of type 2 diabetes, heart disease and other avoidable illnesses. The Glasgow Walking app makes local heritage information easily accessible in the format of curated walks with the aim of getting citizens to tour their city and be more active.

The product allows Glasgow City Council to quickly produce mobile apps aimed at providing information for citizens in a cost effective manner. Apps for both Android and iOS can have data curated on the Glasgow Walking admin and accompanying mobile apps can be quickly reskinned to suit the branding of a given need.

Easy to use web interface to curate and update content

The Glasgow Walking 'Admin area' acts as a centralised single source for the curated heritage information for the city. The council is able to update walks and push updates out to citizens and tourists through the clean and easy to use web interface.

The power of this admin area is in getting information up to date, relevant and useful. In a fast paced modern city such as Glasgow new attractions and activities open on a regular basis.

The admin area has been used throughout the council and by key stakeholders in local community groups

Native apps for a cohesive experience

Add Jam led product development on Glasgow Walking with the of to achieving a first class experience on mobile. We believe the experience delivered could only be achieved through native apps for each platform.

On both platforms we have smooth scrolling of our lists which also include a compass for each item. This smoothly points in the direction of each listed attraction. Giving useful and subtle feedback to the user to create a rich user experience

The apps are highly tailored to each platform to ensure they have a look and feel end users will find familiar. So for example on iOS we make use of 'Blur Views' which are in keeping with the look and feel for iOS introduced with iOS7. On Android we use a styled dialog modal to display the same information as is the norm on Android.
